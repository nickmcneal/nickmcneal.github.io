Hello! I am a third-year undergraduate Computer Science major at The Ohio State University. My research interests include computational neuroscience, machine learning, and decision science. I love reading and learning about human brains, artificial intelligence, and the world in between. I intend on pursuing a graduate education following the completion of my undergraduate degree.

In addition, I am an Eminence Fellow and a Stamps Scholar. Feel free to read about my Eminence group's environmental service organization here.

Since May 2020, I have been leading a computational musicality analysis project in Professor Tai Sing Lee's computer science and neuroscience lab at Carnegie Mellon University. At my home institution, I am advised by Professors Huan Sun and Yu Su in the OSU NLP lab, where I am running some biomedical NLP experiments. I also help out in Professor Ian Krajbich's lab, where I am leading a project that analyzes the relationship between option value and discriminability.

I help with the coding for some experiments in the OSU Music Cognition lab, led by Professor Daniel Shanahan. I also started some machine learning models for the NSF-backed Autonomous Computing and Memory Materials group (ACAMM).
